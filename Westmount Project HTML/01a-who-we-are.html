The Applicant

Dawar Zada, PREC

30+ years in Port Moody. Licensed realtor. Husband, father of three children under six, caretaker to an aging father. Chose the Glenayre neighbourhood to put down roots with his wife, kids, two cats, and a dog — with the goal of building a home his family could grow into.

In 2021, Dawar purchased a development site at 1006 Westmount Drive for $2,125,000 and moved his young family in — expecting to live there for one year while permits were processed, then rent nearby during construction. That was nearly four years ago.

The only way to make the purchase work was to subdivide and build two homes — selling one to afford the other. A coach house for his aging father. A rental suite to help cover costs. Not ideal, but what was available.

Then Bill 44 came and changed everything. Watching the success SSMUH was having in Vancouver — which had adopted the rules a year prior — Dawar saw an opportunity to build something better: four modest homes on one lot, exactly as the Province intended.
