Summary

A family has spent five years and over $250,000 trying to build a home for elderly parents and young children on a property the Province mandated be available for exactly this type of housing. Staff applied half of the relevant bylaw provision and omitted the half that would have helped. Comparable properties received accommodation; this one did not. The applicant discovered every available remedy himself. The housing has not been built. Port Moody has a compliance deadline of June 30, 2026. The housing minister is watching. The five written responses requested in this document are the minimum required to continue working through the City's process rather than around it.

What This Is Not

This is not an attack on the individuals involved. This is a formal record of a process that failed a family trying to build their home, presented to the officials with the authority to correct it. The applicant would still prefer to resolve this here. He is simply no longer pretending that resolution is not also available elsewhere — and that those pathways will not be used if this meeting does not produce results.
