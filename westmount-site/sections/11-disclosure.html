Process Failure

Critical Information Never Proactively Disclosed

What Was Never Disclosed — and Who Found It

The grade-based exception in Bylaw 2937 Section 6.8, the Board of Variance pathway under Local Government Act Section 536, the three-part hardship test, the case-by-case flexibility provisions in the City's own SSMUH Guide, and the Province's Bill 25 consistency obligations were all identified by Dawar Zada through his own research. Staff referenced the Development Variance Permit process only after being asked directly — and when they did, the framing was immediately discouraging: "That's not a variance that staff would support."

"I've reviewed the zoning bylaws and the new guidelines. From what I can tell, lane access is encouraged and available, but it's not mandated. And then it even goes on to say that dual access can be achieved where there is site-specific hardships."
Dawar Zada · February 17, 2026 — citing Bylaw 2937 Section 6.8's grade exception from memory
The applicant cited the correct provision. Moments later, the Manager of Engineering said: "I have to look at the wording of the zoning bylaw again." The applicant knew the bylaw better than the enforcement officer. He found the exception himself. It was never disclosed to him.
