Internal Contradictions

The City's Positions, Placed Side by Side

The City's own documents contradict each other. These are not interpretations. Each pair is from the same source — or two sources that cannot both be correct.

Contradiction I — The Staff Letter Contradicts Itself

What the Letter Acknowledges
"Construction of an access from the laneway is limited [by the retained trees]."
Crystal Wickey, Development Services · Staff comments letter · October 8, 2025
What the Same Letter Requires
A single driveway from the lowest classified road — Grouse Lane — is the only permitted access. Front driveway access from Westmount Drive is not supported by staff.
Crystal Wickey, Development Services · Same letter · October 8, 2025
The Circular Argument — Built into the Official Record

The City acknowledges in writing that lane access is limited by trees. The City simultaneously requires lane-only access and instructs the applicant to prove lane access is infeasible before seeking relief. The City required the tree retention that limits the lane access. The limitation the City created is now the condition the applicant must overcome. This is circular — and it appears in the official file.

Contradiction II — Published Guide vs. Actual Practice

City SSMUH Guide · July 4, 2025
"Relaxation of the regulations in the City of Port Moody Zoning Bylaw, 2018, No. 2937 may be possible on a case-by-case basis to facilitate meaningful tree retention by way of the Development Permit or Development Variance Permit processes."
Port Moody Guide to SSMUH · July 4, 2025
Staff — On the Record, December 16, 2025
"Our messaging for all new developments has been for the maximum number of parking stalls and only the one driveway."
Crystal Wickey · December 16, 2025 · Planning meeting transcript
One is the City's published policy. The other is what staff are actually doing. These two positions cannot coexist.

Contradiction III — Half a Bylaw Applied, Half Omitted

The Rule Staff Applied
"A single driveway access between 3–6m is permitted from the lowest classified road (Grouse Lane)."
Bylaw 2937, Section 6.8 — cited in every staff communication on this file
The Exception in the Same Section — Not Applied
"An alternative access can be provided to a local street where lane access is not feasible due to grade changes."
Bylaw 2937, Section 6.8 — same provision — exception never referenced in any staff communication
The Legal Problem

A bylaw that contains an explicit exception has not been applied as written when the exception is never assessed and never referenced. Staff cited half of Section 6.8 and omitted the other half in every communication. The applicant discovered the exception through his own reading of the bylaw. No staff member raised it in any meeting, letter, or email — despite it being in the same sentence structure as the rule they enforced.
