The Standard on Westmount Drive

Every home on Westmount Drive has access off of Westmount. Six have dual access — including one currently under construction. No homes on this street have only lane access. Yet staff insist that 1006 Westmount, at the highest point on the street with the steepest grade, must be the first.

A project off Mount Royal Drive was directed to lane-only access. The result: 11 vehicles between two homes, most parked on the street and in the neighbouring lane. It has created a blind corner and caused two accidents. It is the biggest complaint in the neighbourhood. This is what lane-only access produces — and staff want to make it the norm.

The Street Doesn't Support Lane-Only Access — And Neither Do the Neighbours

The City's insistence on lane-only access at 1006 Westmount doesn't just create an engineering nightmare — it contradicts the established standard of the street itself. The majority of homes on Westmount Drive access directly from the street. Dual access — front from Westmount, rear from Grouse Lane — is the logical, safe, and consistent approach for this lot. Forcing lane-only access onto a property with a 20.8-foot grade drop is not a standard being applied elsewhere on this street.

Residents on both sides agree. Homeowners along Westmount Drive and Grouse Lane have come together in opposition to the City's position — not against the project, but against the access restriction that makes no practical sense. They want this housing built. They just want it built safely and sensibly.
