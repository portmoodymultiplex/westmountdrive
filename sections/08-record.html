Verbatim Transcript Record

What Staff Said. In Their Own Words.

Four separate statements. All on the record. All from senior City staff. None of them defensible. The following are verbatim quotations from recorded transcripts of the December 16, 2025 and February 17, 2026 planning meetings.

"Looking at something very similar to my own property where I'm not permitted access because I'm on a collector. So it'll be exactly the same where we have a structure in the back and there's a 20 foot drop across my lot and the back unit's gonna have to walk down all the stairs. So, I mean, it happens. Yeah, it's the way it is."
Stephen Judd · Manager of Engineering, City of Port Moody · February 17, 2026
The senior official responsible for enforcing the access bylaw compared the accessibility barrier facing elderly residents and children in a mandated housing development to a personal property inconvenience — and concluded it "happens" and "it's the way it is."
"It's not like there is a hardship from the orientation of the lot. It's more of what you're proposing, like the design you have in mind."
Michael Olubiyi · Manager of Planning, City of Port Moody · February 17, 2026
A surveyed 20.8-foot topographic grade differential — documented in architectural drawings on file with the City — was characterized as a design choice rather than a physical condition of the land. Grade is not a design choice. It is a surveyed geological fact.
"I'm just trying to think. I have to look at the wording of the zoning bylaw again, what it says about access."
Stephen Judd · Manager of Engineering · February 17, 2026
The Manager of Engineering — the senior staff member enforcing the access driveway bylaw — was unable to cite the provision he was applying during a meeting called specifically to discuss that provision's application. Minutes earlier, the applicant had cited it from memory, including its grade-based exception.
"Our messaging for all new developments because there are other SSMUH developments in the neighbourhood has been for the maximum number of parking stalls and only the one driveway. You're not the first application who's come in requesting additional driveways."
Crystal Wickey · Development Services, City of Port Moody · December 16, 2025
A direct, on-record admission that the City is applying a blanket uniform policy to all SSMUH applications — contrary to the case-by-case flexibility in the City's own SSMUH Guide (July 4, 2025) and contrary to Bylaw 2937 Section 6.8's grade exception.
"At one point I was told: daylight the ditch, all parking up Westmount, no access off the lane. And now I'm told the exact opposite."
Dawar Zada · February 17, 2026
This statement was not disputed by any staff member present. The instruction history of this file is the polar opposite of the current requirement. No written rationale for the reversal has ever been provided.
