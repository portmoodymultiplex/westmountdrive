Why This Matters

A Community That Needs This Housing

“We'll accept applications, but it's not something we'd be advertising. It's not something we wanted to see.” — City planning staff to Dawar Zada, regarding SSMUH

The Glenayre Pre-School

When Dawar's four-year-old started pre-K at the Glenayre Centre, he noticed something: out of 13 children in the class, only 3 were from the neighbourhood. The other 10 families were commuting in from outside the area. When he asked why, the answer was the same: lack of suitable housing they could afford. They didn't want aging ranchers. They wanted homes built for modern families.

The Seniors Who Won't Leave

Meanwhile, the seniors who do live in the neighbourhood told Dawar the opposite problem: they don't want condos. They'd rather die in their homes. They're going to extreme lengths to modify aging ranchers just to avoid having to move.

Homes in the Glenayre neighbourhood modified with accessibility ramps — seniors choosing to retrofit rather than leave a community with no suitable alternatives.

Taking It to the People

Dawar took it upon himself to educate the public on what SSMUH could do for Port Moody. Through PortMoodyMultiplex.ca, hosting multiple neighbourhood seminars — where he invited City staff to join — and door-knocking nearly 1,000 homeowners, he started spreading information the City chose not to share.

He was also one of the first applicants under SSMUH in Port Moody. Through his efforts, he now works with approximately 80% of all SSMUH applicants in the city. He has seen their plans, read their comment letters, and heard their struggles firsthand.
