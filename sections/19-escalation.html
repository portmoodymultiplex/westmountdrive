1. Minister of Housing and Municipal Affairs — Province of British Columbia
Christine Boyle, Minister of Housing and Municipal Affairs. Bill 25, passed November 27, 2025, gives the Province explicit authority to monitor and address local government implementation of SSMUH that creates barriers to housing viability. A blanket single-driveway policy applied uniformly to all SSMUH applications, a grade-based exception cited but never assessed, and a family unable to build mandated housing after four years and $250,000 constitute precisely the barrier pattern Bill 25 was enacted to address. The Minister's office will receive this document in full.
2. Board of Variance — City of Port Moody
Local Government Act Section 536. All three elements of the hardship test are met: (a) unique topographic conditions — highest elevation point on the street, city-required retained tree blocking side access, surveyed 20.8 ft grade differential; (b) undue hardship — 34-stair climb for elderly residents and young children; (c) not inappropriate development — four-unit SSMUH, every unit owner-occupied, no speculative component.

Summary

A family has spent five years and over $250,000 trying to build a home for elderly parents and young children on a property the Province mandated be available for exactly this type of housing. Staff applied half of the relevant bylaw provision and omitted the half that would have helped. Comparable properties received accommodation; this one did not. The applicant discovered every available remedy himself. The housing has not been built. Port Moody has a compliance deadline of June 30, 2026. The housing minister is watching. The five written responses requested in this document are the minimum required to continue working through the City's process rather than around it.

What This Is Not

This is not an attack on the individuals involved. This is a formal record of a process that failed a family trying to build their home, presented to the officials with the authority to correct it. The applicant would still prefer to resolve this here. He is simply no longer pretending that resolution is not also available elsewhere — and that those pathways will not be used if this meeting does not produce results.
