One Street. Multiple Failures.

Bankrupt & Sold
1022 Westmount Drive — The Vacant Lot
The MLS record tells the story. Purchased in 2016 for $1,500,000 as a residential detached home. The owner spent years trying to develop it — lost over $600,000 and was driven to the brink of bankruptcy dealing with the City. Eventually listed the bare land: $2,388,000 in April 2022, reduced to $1,998,000, terminated. Re-listed at $1,800,000, terminated again. Re-listed at $1,650,000. Finally sold in November 2022 for $1,500,000 — the same price paid six years earlier, before demolition costs, before professional fees, before a decade of carrying costs.
The new owners are now going through the same process — frustrated, upset, struggling with the same dual access issue, and had no idea they could even argue it. The lot remains vacant. Over a decade of nothing on one street in Port Moody.

1022 Westmount is the cautionary tale. A vacant lot for over a decade. One owner financially ruined. The next owner blindsided by the same barriers. And that is just one property on one block. Developers are now avoiding Port Moody entirely — choosing to build in cities where the process works. This goes directly against the goal of the Province.
